The Problem

Two unsolved problems. One program.

58% of small businesses are trying to adopt AI. They can't afford the talent to do it. Meanwhile, recent graduates have nowhere to build real, verifiable AI experience. The tools are new enough that no one has a real track record — and every resume claims the same thing. Capstone.AI exists because both gaps are real, and they solve each other.

For Businesses

AI consultants charge $200–$500/hr. Hiring a full-time AI specialist means $120K+ before you know if it works. Most small businesses can't access AI capacity at any price that makes sense.

↕

For Talent

Every candidate claims AI skills. None can prove them on a real client with a real outcome. The catch-22: you need experience to get experience. Generic AI familiarity is already table stakes — the credential that matters is documented results.

The Model

How Capstone.AI Works

A structured 90-day engagement with real stakes on both sides. Not a simulation. Not a course. A live project with a qualified client, expert oversight, and documented outcomes.

1

Client Qualification

Businesses apply with a specific, measurable problem. We filter for 2+ years operating, accessible data, and a goal that AI can genuinely move — not just assist with.

2

Team Matching

A trained team of 2–4 is matched to the client based on problem type and domain. The team designs the project scope, defines the baseline metric, and co-signs the deliverable standard.

3

Verified Outcomes

At close, an independent expert panel reviews the work. Outcomes are documented against the baseline. Both client and learner walk away with something verifiable — not just a certificate.

Weeks 1–3

Knowledge Extraction

Structured interviews with the owner. Voice capture. Problem reframing. What's not on the website but should be — pulled out before a single AI tool is used.

Weeks 4–6

Strategy & Architecture

Content strategy, keyword analysis, and a 90-day production plan. The team must defend their strategic choices live — the first moment AI cannot substitute for judgment.

Weeks 7–10

Build & Deploy

The actual work ships. A constraint is introduced at Week 6 — a deliberate curveball requiring teams to adapt. Documented in the portfolio as a Human Judgment Moment.

Weeks 11–12

Measure & Hand Off

Before/after metrics are documented. The AI Leave-Behind Kit — prompt library, voice guide, workflow SOP — is handed to the client. The expert panel evaluates.
